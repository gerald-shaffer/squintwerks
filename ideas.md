# Robert Creek Rock Museum — RockCam Design Ideas

## Context
This is an AR camera app (PWA) for the Robert Creek Rock Museum. The primary interface is a camera viewfinder that detects numbered rocks and plays video narratives. The UI needs to be minimal — it's mostly a camera feed with branded overlay elements. The design should feel like an X-WEB vertical product.

## Idea 1: "Geological Survey Instrument"

**Design Movement:** Scientific instrumentation / vintage field equipment aesthetic
**Core Principles:** Precision, authority, field-ready, utilitarian beauty
**Color Philosophy:** Deep slate blacks with amber/gold instrument markings — like a surveyor's theodolite or vintage compass. The gold represents calibration marks and measurement lines.
**Layout Paradigm:** Full-bleed camera viewfinder with a thin instrument bezel overlay. Corner brackets like a rangefinder camera. Minimal HUD elements positioned at edges.
**Signature Elements:** Corner crosshair brackets that animate when tracking; a thin gold "calibration ring" that appears around detected targets; serif numbering system for exhibits.
**Interaction Philosophy:** Interactions feel like operating precision equipment — smooth, mechanical, deliberate. No bouncy animations.
**Animation:** Slow, precise fades. Crosshairs slide into position with easing. Video panels emerge with a smooth iris-open effect.
**Typography System:** Mono-spaced numerals (JetBrains Mono) for exhibit numbers, paired with a refined serif (Playfair Display) for museum name/titles.

## Idea 2: "Living Stone — Organic Minimalism"

**Design Movement:** Japanese Wabi-sabi meets brutalist minimalism
**Core Principles:** Imperfection as beauty, raw materiality, negative space as content, quiet confidence
**Color Philosophy:** Warm stone grays and off-whites with a single accent of oxidized copper (the rust tones visible on the actual rocks). The palette is drawn directly from the rock surfaces themselves.
**Layout Paradigm:** The camera IS the interface. Almost no chrome. A single floating pill-shaped status indicator. Content emerges from the edges only when needed, then recedes.
**Signature Elements:** A subtle stone-texture grain overlay on UI panels; rounded organic shapes (not geometric); the museum name rendered as if carved/etched.
**Interaction Philosophy:** Zen-like calm. Nothing demands attention. The rock speaks when ready. UI appears and disappears like breath.
**Animation:** Slow dissolves (800ms+). Elements drift in from below like sediment settling. No sharp cuts.
**Typography System:** A single weight of a humanist sans (Instrument Sans) at large sizes with generous letter-spacing. Numbers rendered in a hand-drawn style.

## Idea 3: "X-WEB Network Terminal"

**Design Movement:** Broadcast media / television network control room aesthetic
**Core Principles:** Professional broadcast infrastructure, channel identity, network authority, content-first framing
**Color Philosophy:** True black (#000) background with the X-WEB gold (#C9A84C) as the primary accent — matching the x-web.io site exactly. White text for readability. This is a branded network product.
**Layout Paradigm:** Camera viewfinder framed like a broadcast monitor. A persistent bottom bar shows the channel identity (Robert Creek Rock Museum). Top bar shows network branding (X-WEB). The video plays in a "broadcast window" overlay.
**Signature Elements:** The X-WEB gold horizontal rules; a "LIVE" indicator when scanning; channel identification lower-third that slides in when video plays; the DOT system branding.
**Interaction Philosophy:** Feels like tuning into a channel. Scanning = tuning. Finding the target = signal lock. Video playing = broadcast received.
**Animation:** Sharp, broadcast-style transitions. Lower-thirds slide in from left. Status indicators blink. Channel lock has a brief "signal acquired" flash.
**Typography System:** Condensed sans-serif (Barlow Condensed) for network branding and labels, paired with a clean geometric sans (DM Sans) for body text. All-caps for system status.
